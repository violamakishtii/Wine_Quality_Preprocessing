The WineQT dataset contains 1,143 records and 13 features, including physicochemical properties of red wine such as acidity, pH, sulphates, alcohol content, and a quality score ranging from 3 to 8. Upon inspection, the dataset had no missing values and no duplicate rows, which indicates it was already relatively clean before preprocessing began. However, outlier analysis using the IQR method revealed the presence of extreme values across several features, most notably in residual sugar, chlorides, free sulfur dioxide, total sulfur dioxide, and sulphates — all of which were capped using Winsorization to prevent them from skewing model training without completely discarding the records.
During feature engineering, two new variables were introduced: total_acidity, which combines fixed and volatile acidity into a single measure, and alcohol_binned, a categorical grouping of alcohol content into low, medium, and high tiers. Correlation analysis with the target variable quality revealed that alcohol had the strongest positive relationship, followed by sulphates and citric acid, while volatile acidity showed the strongest negative correlation — meaning wines with higher volatile acidity tended to score lower in quality. This aligns well with domain knowledge, as volatile acidity (acetic acid) contributes to an unpleasant vinegar-like taste. The density and pH features showed relatively weak correlations with quality, suggesting they contribute less predictive value on their own. No features were removed by the variance threshold filter, meaning all variables carried sufficient variance to be considered informative.
After scaling with StandardScaler, all features were normalized to a mean of 0 and standard deviation of 1, ensuring that no single feature would dominate model training due to differences in scale — for example, total sulfur dioxide values in the range of 6–289 compared to pH values between 2.7 and 4.0. The final dataset was split into an 80/20 train-test ratio with stratification on the quality label, resulting in a training set of approximately 914 samples and a test set of around 229 samples, with class proportions preserved across both splits. Overall, the preprocessing pipeline effectively cleaned, enriched, and prepared the data for downstream machine learning tasks, with alcohol content and volatile acidity emerging as the most influential features for predicting wine quality.
